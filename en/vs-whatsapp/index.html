Direct answer

The real cost of WhatsApp for your practice

It's not about money. It's about energy. Every manually sent confirmation, every reminder written on a Sunday evening, every rescheduling negotiated through messages — that's energy taken from somewhere else. From a session, from a weekend, from actual downtime.

It's also a boundary problem. Patients who have your personal number can write at any time. And many do — not for emergencies, but for a quick question, a reschedule, a confirmation they could have sent on Thursday. When there is no system, the patient improvises. And you pay.

APONIA.ro does not replace therapeutic communication. It replaces the logistics: confirmations, reminders, rescheduling. You stay available for what matters. The system handles the rest.

Frequently asked questions

My clients prefer WhatsApp. Will they use a new system?

Experience shows patients adapt quickly when the system is simple. A booking link is easier than a conversation. And they receive confirmation instantly — not after you read the message.

What happens if clients still message me on WhatsApp?

You can politely redirect: "for appointments, please use the link in your email." The habit change takes 1-2 months. Many therapists choose to keep WhatsApp only for real emergencies, not for logistics.

Is it illegal to use WhatsApp for communication with patients?

Not strictly illegal, but concerning from a GDPR perspective. Meta processes metadata from conversations (when, with whom, how long). If the content discussed has a clinical or medical character, storing it on non-EU platforms raises legitimate questions about consent and data transfers.

Does APONIA.ro send notifications via WhatsApp or SMS?

APONIA.ro sends notifications via SMS and email. These come from the system's number/address — not from your personal number. Clients receive reminders without any manual involvement from you.

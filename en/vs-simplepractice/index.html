Why an American product doesn't solve a European problem

SimplePractice is built for a specific context: American therapist, American patients, American insurance, American laws. The product is mature and well-designed. But your context is different.

As a therapist in Europe, you have clear GDPR obligations regarding your patients' data. SimplePractice is HIPAA-compliant, not GDPR-compliant. That doesn't mean it's illegal to use, but it means GDPR compliance responsibility stays entirely with you — with no native support from the platform you're paying $59 a month for.

Furthermore: you're paying for telehealth integrated with American insurers, for English-language forms, for a billing system adapted to US CPT codes. Features you simply cannot use in a European context.

APONIA.ro is built exactly for your context — a practice in Europe, patients with European expectations, data that must stay in the EU. No excess, no paying for features you cannot access.

Frequently asked questions

SimplePractice has GDPR compliance — why do you say it doesn't?

SimplePractice offers a DPA (Data Processing Agreement) for European customers, which is a step. But the platform is built on AWS infrastructure in the USA, with internal processes designed for HIPAA. Real GDPR compliance — right to erasure, data portability, 72-hour breach notification — is not their core business.

SimplePractice has more features. Isn't it better?

It depends on your practice. A solo therapist in Europe doesn't need insurance billing, e-prescribe, superbills, or multi-therapist group sessions. You pay for features you never use. APONIA.ro is exactly what you need — nothing more.

Can I migrate from SimplePractice to APONIA.ro?

Yes. APONIA.ro supports import from standard CSV exports. More complex session notes can be transferred manually or in bulk during onboarding.

Will APONIA.ro offer SimplePractice's advanced features?

Features relevant to the European market, yes. Those specific to the American healthcare system, no. APONIA.ro focuses on what matters for a European practice: scheduling, session notes, patient communication, GDPR compliance.

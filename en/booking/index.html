The problem

The confirmation chain that eats your week

"Are you free Thursday at 6?" — "Yes, Thursday at 6 works." — "OK, confirmed, see you then." Multiplied by every session, every patient, every week.

At 10 sessions a week, that's 20-30 messages just for confirmations. Add rescheduling and cancellations, and you're spending a real chunk of your evenings on logistics you didn't plan for.

The deeper problem: the confirmation burden grows with every patient you add. It's a tax on success.

Common questions

Can I set different hours for different days?

Yes. You define availability per day independently. Mondays 14:00-20:00, Thursdays 10:00-18:00, Fridays not available — whatever your schedule is. You can also adjust it week by week.

Can I block vacation days or emergency closures?

Yes. Block any date range with one click. Patients cannot book those days. Existing bookings are not affected — they stay in place unless you move them manually.

What happens with last-minute cancellations?

You set your own cancellation policy — for example, no cancellations under 24 hours before the session. Patients who try to cancel within that window see your policy automatically. How you handle late cancellations is your call; the system just enforces your rules.

What if my patients aren't comfortable with technology?

The patient flow is: click your link, pick a slot, enter name and email, done. No account to create, no app to install, no login to remember. It is simpler than booking a restaurant online.

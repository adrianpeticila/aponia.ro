The real problem

The patchwork that grows with every patient

Google Calendar for scheduling. WhatsApp for messages. A notebook for session notes. A spreadsheet for payments. Each tool works individually. Together, they require a human coordinator to keep them synchronized. That coordinator is you.

With 5 patients, the patchwork is manageable. With 12, you're spending a real chunk of your evenings on logistics. With 20, something starts falling through the cracks.

The patchwork doesn't solve itself. It grows. APONIA.ro replaces it with one system that handles everything except the therapy.

Can they work together? Yes. APONIA.ro has a Google Calendar integration. Your APONIA.ro bookings appear in Google Calendar automatically. You can use both — APONIA.ro as your practice system, Google Calendar as your personal view.

A note on GDPR

Clinical data in a personal Google account is not the same as compliant storage

Article 9 of the GDPR classifies health data as a special category — the highest protection level. Appointment notes, session details, patient names paired with session dates — these are health data.

Storing them in a personal Google Calendar account means they're governed by Google's consumer privacy policy, not a data processing agreement designed for health data processors. The difference is small until it matters — a data subject request, a breach, or an audit.

APONIA.ro stores all data in Frankfurt, Germany, under EU jurisdiction, with AES-256 encryption and a data processing agreement built for Article 9 compliance.

Common questions

Can APONIA.ro sync with Google Calendar?

Yes. Connect your Google account and your APONIA.ro bookings automatically appear in Google Calendar. You keep your familiar calendar view while APONIA.ro handles the practice management side.

I already use Google Calendar and it works fine. Is switching worth it?

If you have fewer than 8 patients and admin feels manageable, probably not. The value of a dedicated practice system shows when patient volume grows and the patchwork — different apps for notes, payments, messages — gets harder to maintain manually.

Is the GDPR risk actually real for a small practice?

Yes. The regulation applies regardless of practice size. The practical risk is small for most therapists most of the time — until there's a data subject request, a complaint to the data protection authority, or a breach. At that point, "I was using my personal Google account" is not a compliant answer.

How long does migrating take?

Under one hour. Import your patient list via CSV, set your availability, share your new booking link. Existing appointments can be entered manually or left in Google Calendar until they pass — there's no hard cutover required.

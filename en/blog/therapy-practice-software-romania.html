Therapy practice software in Romania: a comparison guide (2026)

8 min read  ·  June 2026

A neutral guide. It lists the real tools independent psychotherapists use in Romania, what each one does and who it suits. One of them is APONIA.ro. We say so upfront, so you read the rest knowing that.

If you run a psychotherapy practice on your own, software is not the hard part of your work. The hard part is the session. Software should just let you reach it without losing an hour a day to scheduling, rescheduling, reminders and invoices.

The problem is that "practice software" means very different things in Romania. Some tools do everything: COPSI registers, e-Invoicing, accounting, psychological assessments. Others do one thing, but do it well. Before you test five platforms and lose a week, here is what each one does.

Why not just WhatsApp and Google Calendar

It's tempting. The client is already on WhatsApp, they don't have to install anything, you write "Thursday at 5?" and you're done. It works until it doesn't.

Two problems build up quietly. The first is operational: without real sync, automatic reminders and a separation between personal and professional, you end up negotiating hours instead of holding sessions, and no-shows climb. The second is compliance. As a therapist you are the data controller over the most sensitive category that exists, mental health data. A reminder sent from a personal WhatsApp account processes health data on a consumer app, without an adequate processing agreement. This is not theoretical: EU data protection authorities have already fined medical providers for exactly this.

The conclusion is not "WhatsApp is banned". It's "WhatsApp isn't built for this, and with health data the risk is yours, not Meta's". That's where the need for a dedicated tool begins.

In short, what each one does

APONIA.ro. It does one thing and keeps it simple: scheduling. Calendar, automatic reminders, a booking link the client uses without an account, payment. The idea behind it is that a good therapist shouldn't become their own administrative assistant. It's not an ERP with dozens of modules, and that's deliberate. If you want COPSI registers, accounting and assessments in a single dashboard, APONIA.ro isn't for you. If you want to stop thinking about your calendar, it is.

ePsihologie (ePsi). It covers a lot: calendar with an invoice issued directly from the session, reminders, Google Meet/Zoom integration, COPSI registers, accounting and the single tax return. It's for those who want everything under one roof and accept the complexity that comes with it.

eCabinet. COPSI registers, ANAF e-Invoicing, online scheduling and a public catalog of psychologists, starting, according to its site, from 49 RON/month. Good value for those who also want the compliance side and a bit of visibility.

CabinetFlow. Automates invoicing through Oblio and payments through NETOPIA, with scheduling synced to Google Calendar. The logical choice if you already work in Oblio.

PsihoManager. Specialized in psychological assessments (occupational, transport, work), e-Invoicing and GDPR contracts. If a large part of your work is assessments rather than just therapy, this is its niche.

PsihoERP and Easy Liberal. More complete toolkits, up to automations, for practices that want a comprehensive system and are willing to invest time in setup.

How to choose, without testing five platforms

The question isn't "which is best". It's "what hurts the most".

  • Only the calendar hurts (scheduling, rescheduling, no-shows, reminders) and the rest is handled by an accountant? Get a tool that does scheduling well and nothing extra. That's where APONIA.ro fits.
  • Compliance hurts (COPSI registers, e-Invoicing, accounting) and you want it all in one place? Look at ePsihologie or eCabinet.
  • You issue psychological assessments as a big part of your work? PsihoManager is built for exactly that.
  • You already keep accounting in Oblio? CabinetFlow plugs straight in.

The simple rule: don't buy an ERP to solve a calendar problem. You'd pay monthly for ten modules to use one. And don't buy a plain calendar if your real problem is that ANAF and COPSI are on your back. Match the tool to the pain, not to the feature list.

Frequently asked questions

What software do independent psychotherapists use in Romania?

The most common are APONIA.ro (focused on scheduling), ePsihologie, eCabinet, CabinetFlow, PsihoManager, PsihoERP and Easy Liberal. They differ in how much they cover: some only scheduling, others the entire administrative cycle.

Is it legal to use WhatsApp for therapy appointments?

It is not banned, but with health data the compliance risk is yours. A personal WhatsApp account does not provide an adequate framework for processing sensitive data. A dedicated tool, with a data processing agreement and EU hosting, moves you out of the grey zone.

How much does therapy practice software cost?

It varies. Some start at around 49 RON/month, APONIA.ro is 73 RON/month, and full suites require a custom quote. For a small practice, the monthly cost is less than a single session.

Do I need a full system or just scheduling?

It depends on what's slowing you down. If your tax admin is handled and you only lose time on the calendar, a dedicated scheduling tool is enough. If you also struggle with registers and invoicing, you need a broader platform.

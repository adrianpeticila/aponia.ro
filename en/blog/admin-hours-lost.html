How many hours a week are you losing to admin?
(And how to get them back)

5 min read  ·  April 2026

We asked 14 psychotherapists how many hours a week they spend on administrative tasks. Not on therapy. On confirming appointments, replying to messages, managing rescheduling, tracking payments, keeping notes.

The average answer: 4-6 hours per week.

None of it was therapy. None of it was paid.

At an average session rate of 200 RON, 264 hours represents 52,800 RON not earned annually — or more precisely, not earned. Time that could have been therapy, rest, or simply something else.

What those hours actually contain

It's not one single block of lost time. It's a mosaic of small interruptions, spread across the day and evening:

Confirmations and re-confirmations. "Are you free Thursday at 6?" "Thursday at 6 is free, but Tuesday at 5 is taken." Back and forth, several times, for every new patient and every rescheduling.

Last-minute cancellations. Managing the response, rescheduling, potentially following up on the late-cancellation fee — if you have a policy, which isn't always the case.

Notes and records. Where are the notes from Tuesday's session? When did patient X last pay? What did we discuss last time? Without a system, answering these questions costs minutes every time.

"I spent well over an hour a week confirming appointments, answering availability questions, handling rescheduling. An hour that isn't therapy and isn't paid."

Why it grows with every patient you add

With 5-6 active patients, admin is manageable. A notebook, a calendar, WhatsApp. It works.

From 10-12 patients upward, the old system stops scaling. Every new patient doesn't just add a session — they add a communication relationship, a message channel, a series of coordinations. The chaos doesn't grow linearly. It grows exponentially.

And precisely when your practice is going well — when you have more patients, more income — you spend more time on admin. It's a penalty for success.

What recovery actually looks like

Recovery doesn't mean working faster or being more organised. It means eliminating tasks instead of optimising them.

The patient books themselves, from your link, without a single message first. You get a notification. Done. You confirmed nothing.

The 24h and 1h reminder goes out automatically. You wrote nothing.

The patient cancels themselves from their booking, if it's more than X hours in advance. The slot frees up automatically. You managed nothing.

Notes are in the same place as the appointments. You open the patient's profile and find them immediately.

It's not a discipline question. It's a system that does things automatically and gives back a few hours per week that you could spend differently.

4-6 hours per week. 22 per month. 264 per year. How many more do you want to lose?
